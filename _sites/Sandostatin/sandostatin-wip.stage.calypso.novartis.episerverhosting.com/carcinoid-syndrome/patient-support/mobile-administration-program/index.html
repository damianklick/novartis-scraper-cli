Important Safety Information and Approved Use

Warnings and Precautions: Treatment with Sandostatin LAR Depot may affect gallbladder function, with postmarketing reports of gallstones resulting in complications (inflammation of the gallbladder, bile duct, and pancreas, and requiring surgical removal of the gallbladder); sugar metabolism; thyroid and heart function; and nutritional absorption, which may require monitoring by your doctor. Call your doctor if you experience signs or symptoms of gallstones or any of their complications.

Before Taking Sandostatin LAR Depot: Tell your doctor if you have a history of heart disease or are taking other medications, including cyclosporine, insulin, oral hypoglycemic agents, beta-blockers, and bromocriptine.

Common Side Effects: Most patients experience side effects at some time. Some common side effects you may experience include back pain, fatigue, headache, abdominal pain, nausea, and dizziness.

Other Information: Patients with carcinoid tumors and VIPomas should adhere closely to their scheduled return visits for reinjection in order to minimize exacerbation of symptoms.

You are encouraged to report negative side effects of prescription drugs to the FDA. Visit www.fda.gov/medwatch, or call 1-800-FDA-1088.

Please see full Prescribing Information.

Approved Use

Sandostatin® LAR Depot (octreotide acetate) for injectable suspension is indicated for patients in whom initial treatment with immediate-release Sandostatin® (octreotide acetate) Injection has been shown to be effective and tolerated for

  • Long-term treatment of the severe diarrhea and flushing episodes associated with metastatic carcinoid tumors
  • Long-term treatment of the profuse watery diarrhea associated with VIP-secreting tumors

In patients with carcinoid syndrome and VIPomas, the effect of Sandostatin Injection and Sandostatin LAR Depot on tumor size, rate of growth, and development of metastases has not been determined.

At Novartis, we take the health and well-being of patients and health care professionals very seriously. We understand the concern and uncertainty that you may be facing surrounding the novel coronavirus (COVID-19).

 

That's why we are making the Sandostatin LAR Depot Mobile Administration Program (MAP) available, at no charge, for eligible* patients who have difficulty going to their monthly injection appointments because of the ongoing COVID-19 pandemic.

 

Through MAP:

  • The eligible* patient will, at no charge, have a trained health care professional prepare and administer the patient's treatment at a location that is convenient for the patient, such as their home†
  • Administration is available on nights and weekends
  • The enrolling physician will receive a report after each patient administration

Eligibility

  • Patients must be prescribed Sandostatin LAR Depot for an FDA-approved indication
  • During the COVID-19 pandemic, Novartis is temporarily modifying the program to include patients with any insurance type to be eligible

2 Ways for Patients to Enroll in the Program

Option 1 – Health Care Professional-Initiated Enrollment:

 

  • Health care professionals begin enrollment for patients by completing the Sandostatin LAR Depot Service Request Form and faxing it to 1-888-891-4924
  • Novartis Patient Assistance Now Oncology (PANO) will then reach out to patients to complete the enrollment process
  • For more information on PANO processing, health care professionals can visit the PANO HCP Page or call 1-800-282-7630

 

Option 2 – Patient-Initiated Enrollment:

 

  • Patients begin enrollment by completing a PANO Service Request Form
  • PANO will then send health care professionals the provider portion of the form to complete and return
  • Patients can also reach out to PANO by calling 1-800-282-7630

 

Once the patient is enrolled, MAP services will be coordinated between in-home health care professionals and patients.

 

As part of the program, the patient works with their physician and pharmacy to acquire the product and have it shipped to their home. The program will coordinate a visit by a trained health care professional upon receipt of the product by the patient. The health care professional will come to a patient's home to prepare and administer the patient's Sandostatin LAR Depot. It is important to note that not all insurance plans cover having the product administered in a home setting, and therefore, it is important that the patient discusses home administration with their physician and insurance plan.

 

*Limitations apply. Novartis reserves the right to update limitations and eligibility at any time.

 

†The program does not replace professional care or the authority of the prescribing physician. Patients still keep their regularly scheduled physician checkups. First dose must be administered by prescribing physician.
